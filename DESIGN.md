# Design Direction

## Brand Concept

Working brand: **Summit Ridge Powersports**.

The dealership should feel premium, rugged, and useful: a place for buyers comparing motorcycles, ATVs, side-by-sides, and personal watercraft before making a serious purchase. The tone should be confident and direct, with outdoor/adventure energy grounded in retail clarity.

## Visual Principles

- Lead with inventory and terrain, not abstract graphics.
- Use strong photography, crisp contrast, and practical dealership information.
- Favor purposeful density over airy SaaS emptiness.
- Keep the interface clean enough for financing and lead capture to feel trustworthy.
- Make the site feel like a real store with real units, not a landing page mockup.
- Every major section should answer a buyer question: what is available, what does it cost, can I finance it, can I trade, and how do I contact the dealer?

## Color

- Base: warm off-white, charcoal, graphite, and deep asphalt.
- Accents: safety orange, muted trail green, brushed metal, and small hits of electric lime only where useful.
- Avoid dominant purple, blue SaaS gradients, beige monotony, and glossy neon motorsports styling.
- Use dark sections sparingly for contrast and product drama.

## Typography

- Use a modern sans-serif system stack unless a font is intentionally added later.
- Headlines should be strong, compact, and readable.
- Body copy should be direct and dealership-specific.
- Avoid oversized hero text inside compact panels.
- Do not scale type with viewport width.

## Layout

- Home should create immediate dealership credibility with a full-bleed vehicle/terrain image, direct buyer CTAs, and a visible hint of inventory below the fold.
- SRP should prioritize filtering, scanning, and comparison with tighter spacing than a marketing page.
- VDP should prioritize media, year/make/model, price, specs, availability, and lead actions in the first screen on desktop and early on mobile.
- Use full-width bands and constrained inner layouts.
- Cards are for inventory units, lead modules, and repeated content only.
- Do not nest cards inside cards.

## Components

- Inventory cards should show image, condition/year/make/model, price, category, mileage or hours, location/availability, and primary action.
- Filters should be practical: category, condition, make, price, and usage metric where relevant.
- SRP should include result count, sort, active filter chips, clear filters, and a useful empty state.
- CTAs should be action-oriented: view details, check availability, get financed, value trade, call, text, schedule visit.
- Forms should be short and useful, not decorative.
- Use one tasteful dealership-specific motif, such as "Ready for pickup in Summit Ridge" availability chips or category-specific spec rows.

## Mobile UX

- Mobile buyers should be able to browse inventory quickly with sticky or easily reachable filters and large tappable inventory cards.
- CTAs must remain tappable and clear.
- VDP media and lead actions should appear early.
- Avoid hiding important buyer actions below long marketing sections.
- Touch targets should be at least 44px where practical.
- VDP should use a sticky or near-sticky action area for check availability, call/text, financing, and trade-in actions.

## Avoid

- Generic AI hero sections with vague promises.
- Cheesy speed lines, flames, faux carbon fiber, or extreme-sports noise.
- Purple-blue gradient SaaS styling.
- Decorative orbs, bokeh blobs, and abstract SVG filler.
- Empty metrics that do not help a buyer.
- Copy that sounds like a marketing template instead of a dealership.
